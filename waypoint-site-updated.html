The Problem

Most small nonprofits are not short on care. They are short on advancement infrastructure.

Good people can still end up trapped in event-driven fundraising, inconsistent follow-up, weak donor records, and board members who want to help but do not know what help should look like.

Donor Relationships

People give once, then drift.

Not because they stopped caring. Usually because no one built a clear stewardship rhythm after the gift.

Board Engagement

The board wants to help, but the role is fuzzy.

Most volunteers are willing to contribute when the asks are specific, useful, and matched to their actual relationships.

Funding Stability

Events carry too much of the load.

A gala can matter. It should not be the whole funding strategy. The event needs to feed a larger donor journey.

The WMP Lens

Advancement is not one ask. It is the full relationship system around the mission.

  • Cultivation: helping the right people understand the mission before they are asked to fund it.
  • Solicitation: making clear, appropriate asks tied to specific outcomes and real organizational needs.
  • Stewardship: showing donors what happened because they gave, and why their continued involvement matters.
  • Board Enablement: giving volunteers specific roles they can picture themselves doing well.
  • Operating Rhythm: making sure donor work has owners, dates, tools, and follow-up.

FAQ

Common questions before starting.

Is this only for organizations with a development director?

No. It is often most useful for organizations that do not yet have a formal advancement role, because the assessment clarifies what needs to exist before hiring or adding complexity.

Is this a fundraising campaign?

No. WMP can help with campaign structure, but the core work is building the advancement system that makes campaigns, stewardship, board engagement, and donor follow-up work together.

Can this be shared with the board?

Yes. The written assessment is designed to be board-readable. It gives leaders a practical way to talk about advancement without turning the conversation into blame or vague ambition.

What happens after the assessment?

You can implement the recommendations yourself, or we can discuss a deeper engagement to help build the systems. If there is not a fit, we will say that plainly.
