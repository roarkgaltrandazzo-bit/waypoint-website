The Premise

A good mission and committed people are not the same thing as an advancement program.

Most nonprofits, private schools, and faith-based organizations run a few annual events, send a year-end appeal, and rely on a handful of long-time donors who carry too much of the load. That is not a program. That is a pattern held together by goodwill and repetition.

The gap between where you are and where you could be is almost never about the mission, the team, or the donors themselves. It is about the system. How donors are identified and cultivated. How gifts are asked for. How people are stewarded after they give. How the board is equipped to help. How all of it connects.

The assessment is the first step in seeing that system clearly.

Common Questions

Things worth knowing before you sign up.

Why is this priced so low?

The assessment is the beginning of a potential relationship. We price it to be accessible so the decision to do it is not a budget conversation. Our deeper engagements carry different pricing, and we only propose those when the assessment shows a real fit.

Is this going to end in a high-pressure sales pitch?

No. The follow-up conversation is a genuine review of the assessment. If deeper work is a fit, we will say so. If not, we will give you the assessment and wish you well.

Do we need to have our development function figured out before we do this?

No. The assessment is designed to surface what is there and what is not. The less formalized your current function, the more value you will likely get from an honest outside view.

Who conducts the conversation?

The conversation is conducted by our team directly. The assessment itself is written, reviewed, and delivered by a person, not a template.

Can we share the assessment with our board?

Yes. The assessment is written to be shareable. Many of our clients use it as a starting point for a board-level conversation about advancement.
